PRODUCTION TASK

{% if player.role == 'worker' %}

You have been randomly assigned to the role of WORKER in today’s session.

The production task for today's study is known as a "decoding task." Your task is to decode numbers into letters using the decoding keys in the grid provided on the computer screen, as depicted below. In each period, the key will be different.

Below you see a random grid with a decoding key assigning the 26 alphabetic characters to two-digit numbers. Your task consists of “decoding” numbers by filling the corresponding alphabetic character from the decoding key into the free space next to the number that has to be decoded.

Example: 61 would be decoded to D using the decoding key provided below.

{% include 'global/Key.html' %}

Answers are not case sensitive. After you type in the letter you decode, please make sure you click the SUBMIT button to submit the answer. If you do not click the SUBMIT button to submit the answer, the answer will not be recorded and will not count toward your performance.

Across the ten periods, you will experience normal and difficult periods. In a normal period, you will decode one number into one letter in every grid. For example, in one grid, you would get one assigned number, such as 57, which you would decode into L. In a difficult period, you will decode two numbers into two letters in every grid. For example, in one grid, you would get two assigned numbers, such as 36 58, which you would decode into G A. Your performance in each period is calculated as the number of grids you correctly submit. Each period the computer will randomly determine if it is a normal or difficult period, with an 80% likelihood of being a normal period and a 20% likelihood of being a difficult period. The manager will be told if the period was a normal or difficult period at the end of each period.

After you have completed reading the instructions, you will have 60 seconds to practice the decoding task under a normal period.

Note: Your performance in the practice round will NOT be paid, but the practice round provides you a good opportunity to become familiar with the task.

The worker will practice the task, followed by performing the task for ten periods. Across the ten periods, the worker may experience normal and difficult periods. In a normal period, the worker will decode one number into one letter in every grid. For example, in one grid, the worker would get one assigned number, such as 57, which they would decode into L. In a difficult period, the worker will decode two numbers into two letters in every grid. For example, in one grid, the worker would get assigned two numbers, such as 51 59, which they would decode into J M. The worker’s performance in each period is calculated as the number of grids they correctly solve and submit. Their objective is to correctly solve and submit as many grids as possible. Each period the computer will randomly determine if it is a normal or difficult period, with an 80% likelihood of being a normal period and a 20% likelihood of being a difficult period. You will be told if the period was a normal or difficult period at the end of each period.

While the worker performs the decoding task, you will be answering trivia questions. For each correctly answered trivia question, you will earn 20 Lira. You will spend 15 seconds on each trivia question.
